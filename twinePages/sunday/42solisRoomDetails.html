Sunday, dies Solis, “the day of the sun.” Sunday is named for the sun. Sunday comes from Old English “Sunnandæg," which is derived from a Germanic interpretation of the Latin. Germanic and Norse mythology personify the sun as a goddess named Sunna or Sól. The essence of this day is to shine one’s light brightly, t o take the lead, and to share one’s vitality.
